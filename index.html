Memristor Devices & Arrays 忆阻器器件与阵列

My research in memristors focuses on developing highly uniform, scalable, and reconfigurable non-volatile memory devices. By engineering ion profiles and exploiting novel switching mechanisms, we aim to overcome the variability challenges in traditional ReRAM.

Specifically, we have demonstrated the integration of hybrid memristor arrays with multi-phototransistors to create light-tunable weights for optic neuromorphic computing.

在忆阻器研究领域，我致力于开发高度均匀、可扩展且可重构的非易失性存储器件。通过离子轮廓工程和探索新型开关机制，我们旨在克服传统阻变存储器 (ReRAM) 中的可变性挑战。

具体而言，我们展示了混合忆阻器阵列与多光电晶体管的集成，为光学类脑计算创建了光可调权重。

Ferroelectronics & Emerging NVM 铁电电子学与新型非易失性存储器

Ferroelectric materials, particularly HfO2-based thin films (like HZO) and novel nitrides, present a breakthrough for next-generation logic and memory devices. My work involves the growth, characterization, and integration of these materials into advanced device architectures.

A key highlight is the Electric-Field-Induced Phase Transition RAM (EPTRAM) based on 3D nitride materials for 6G and AI applications, providing exceptional thermal robustness and high-speed operation. We also explore HZO/HSO superlattice ReFET arrays for stable NVM applications.

铁电材料，特别是基于二氧化铪的薄膜（如 HZO）和新型氮化物，为下一代逻辑和存储器件带来了突破。我的工作涉及这些材料的生长、表征以及将其集成到先进的器件架构中。

核心亮点是基于 3D 氮化物材料的电场诱导相变存储器 (EPTRAM)，面向 6G 和 AI 应用，提供卓越的热稳定性和高速运行能力。我们还探索了用于稳定 NVM 应用的 HZO/HSO 超晶格 ReFET 阵列。

Neuromorphic Computing Hardware 类脑计算硬件系统

Bridging materials science and AI, my work focuses on designing brain-inspired hardware that can process spatiotemporal data (vision/audio) with extreme energy efficiency.

We have developed hardware implementations of Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) using hybrid memristor arrays featuring fatigue spike-timing-dependent plasticity (F-STDP), enabling adaptive learning at the edge.

作为材料科学与人工智能之间的桥梁，我的工作重点是设计受大脑启发的硬件系统，使其能够以极高的能效处理时空数据（视觉/音频信号）。

我们开发了基于混合忆阻器阵列的脉冲神经网络 (SNN) 硬件实现，利用疲劳脉冲时间依赖可塑性 (F-STDP) 特征，实现了边缘侧的自适应学习。
